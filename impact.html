Our Impact

MaterialBank is proud to support communities through school and art supply donations that help students, families, and youth access the tools they need to thrive.

Toronto

  • On March 16, 2026, we made a donation to the SMILE Canada Foundation. This remarkable organization has supported more than 1,300 families, children, and youth living with disabilities and long term health conditions across Canada. As part of our contribution, we donated 13 backpacks filled with school supplies to help support their families and youth.
  • On October 25, 2024, we visited Horizons for Youth where we donated school and art supplies and over 50 backpacks to help support their student residents. Horizons for Youth provides at-risk and homeless youth ages 16 to 24 in Toronto with holistic support, life changing programs, and a home to help them leave the streets behind.
  • On July 8, 2024, we donated hundreds of school and art supplies to YMCA's Black Achievers Program. Their program provides opportunities for Black high school aged youth in the Greater Toronto Area, allowing students to develop socially and academically through the support of Black mentors and role models.
  • On January 24, 2024, we visited YWCA Toronto’s Girls Centre, where we donated a wide range of school supplies such as binders and textbooks. YWCA Toronto Girls Centre provides mental health, educational, and leadership programs to help students thrive academically. Their programs range from university assistance to building community leaders through unique opportunities to engage, fundraise, and advocate for global issues.
  • On November 29, 2023, we went to CAMH, the Centre for Addiction and Mental Health, and donated art supplies ranging from marker kits to oil pastels. These supplies supported CAMH’s Gifts of Light program, which is fully donation funded. Their mission is to identify patient wellness gaps and create change by working with patients and families, providing access to basic necessities and activities that support transition back into the community.
  • On October 25, 2023, we donated to Princess Margaret Cancer Centre’s childcare program, the Magic Castle. The donation included school and art supplies ranging from washable paint to packs of crayons, which are used during arts and crafts activities for children in the program.
  • On August 9, 2023, we visited Native Child and Family Services of Toronto and donated supplies ranging from pens to backpacks for their back to school initiative. The organization supports children and families through programs focused on quality of life, wellbeing, caring, healing, and education.
  • On August 9, 2023, we had the pleasure of donating to Kids of Incarcerated Parents (KIP Canada). We donated over 1,000 school supplies ranging from erasers to binders to help students access the supplies they need for back to school. This organization provides support and guidance for children whose families are affected by the justice system.
  • On August 8, 2023, we visited Eastview Neighbourhood Community Centre where we donated a wide range of goods, from erasers to binders. These donations were used in their after school program and back to school initiative. The centre provides programs for students of all ages.
  • On May 31, 2023, we completed our second donation. In total, we donated 7 bags with over 2,000 items including paint, paper, workbooks, pens, and more. Toronto Foundation for Student Success recognizes that many young people in Toronto do not have access to after school programs, so their Beyond 3:30 program creates a safe and welcoming space for children ages 8 to 14 to learn, play, eat, and grow.
  • On May 26, 2023, we completed our first donation of art supplies, including pencils, pens, and stickers, to Friends of Ruby. These supplies were used in their art programs as a welcoming space for 2SLGBTQIA+ youth in Toronto. Friends of Ruby is a drop in centre for youth across the GTA, providing support programs and essentials.

Boston

  • On November 9, 2023, we went to Boston to donate to Christopher’s Haven and the Boston House of Hope and Healing. We donated school and art supplies ranging from crayons to sketchbooks to support children and families.

Jamaica

  • In April 2024, our donations made it to Chapelton Primary School in Clarendon, Jamaica. We partnered with Roots to Routes Academy to donate an assortment of school and art supplies.
  • Reflection: “Some students, without a doubt, didn’t have the luxuries of money. The backpacks, I felt, allowed the students to feel special and to feel confident.”
